Solid 26 is the first annual release of the Solid specification, establishing a stable, versioned foundation that developers and enterprises can plan and build against with confidence. For the first time, it pins stable versions of the component specifications, ending uncertainty for organisations seeking to commit to the technology — and ships with consistent tooling and training materials, replacing a previously scattered set of resources.

As AI services accumulate ever more personal data, Solid provides the infrastructure for people to decide which services can access their data and on what terms. Unlike current personal AI agents, which typically require broad and largely unrestricted access to a person's files, Solid's permission system allows users to specify not just who can access their data but what a service is permitted, prohibited, or obligated to do with it — for example, permitting personalisation while prohibiting use for machine-learning training.

Solid also solves a problem that has long held back the market for personal-data applications: the absence of a common standard for cloud storage that third-party developers can build with. Today, any developer building an application that reads or writes personal data must build separate integrations for every provider. Solid provides a single standard that works across all compatible providers, making an open, interoperable market possible for the first time.

Solid 26 is a community-endorsed step towards a future 1.0 release, with a structured programme — including working groups spanning enterprise, government, research, social good, individual users, and AI — to gather real-world feedback throughout 2026 and shape what comes next. The Open Data Institute (ODI), steward of Solid since October 2024, ensures the technology develops in ways that serve people and society, not just commercial interests.

Get Involved

We need your support and feedback on Solid26: The ODI is organising a serires of engagement exercises with interested volunteers across many fields. Interested participants will be added to a sectoral group, asked to familiarise themselves with Solid26, then identify and prioritise adoption challenges for Solid in their area of expertise. This feedback will be used to steer efforts in further developing the protocol.

If you would like to participate in these exercises, please reach out to Roberto S.K Breitman (ODI Solid Community Manager) with a short message detailing your field of expertise, and your experience within that field so he can help you find the appropriate feedback group.
